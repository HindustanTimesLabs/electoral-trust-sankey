Electoral Trusts: How some of India’s biggest companies route money to political parties

By Harry Stevens and Aman Sethi

12 April, 2017

In recent years, electoral trust companies have emerged as a major source of funding for political parties. For the corporations that finance them, electoral trusts give an impression of an arms-length transaction, creating a buffer that allows companies to give money to parties without appearing to favour one over the other.

In FY 2015-16, General Electoral Trust stopped making donations, and a trust called Samaj Electoral Trust, funded by a raft of companies with addresses linked to the Birla Group, began making contributions.

Some of the country’s largest and most influential corporate conglomerates — from Bharti and Birla to Tata and Torrent — bankroll these trusts. Together, they represent a broad swath of corporate India invested in highly regulated sectors where seemingly minor changes in policy can have a disproportionate impact on balance sheets.
